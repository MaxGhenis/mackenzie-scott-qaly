Resolution of round-1 findings

Round-1 finding Status Note
1 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The manuscript now says the multipliers are designed to partition doubt and explicitly concedes mixed null/positive evidence, omitted harm, and imperfect separation (rendered lines 164–168, 701–716), but it still provides neither the requested archetype-level ledger nor one-factor-off runs. Deferral is defensible for an SSRN working paper because the overlap is now prominent, but it is not a completed audit.
2 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The exact concentration (60), an implied global-health interval, the judgment-prior interpretation, and the omitted pipeline uncertainty are now stated (lines 168–176, 716–718); no concentration sweep, fixed-share case, bootstrap, or hierarchical propagation is reported. Deferral is defensible for a working paper now that the interval is not presented as sampling uncertainty.
3 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The selected sample, sample flow, repeated-gift treatment, pooled years, 287 median fallbacks, and lack of prediction validation are disclosed (lines 104–113), but there is still no clustered/robust inference, held-out validation, influence analysis, or propagation of elasticity/fallback uncertainty. Deferral is defensible only because the fit is now expressly descriptive and conditional; the abstract/contribution language should retain that qualification.
4 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The caption gives the exact endpoint means and calls the curve a scenario rather than a bound, and the dominant bucket is correctly attributed (lines 601–615). However, the introduction still says “counting only randomized evidence,” the plotted ticks still say “RCT-only” and “face value,” and the limitations call the sweep an “honest bound” (lines 51–52, 594–604, 701–703).
5 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The false common-convention claim has been replaced by an accurate source-specific account for the default run (lines 177–187). Declining to overwrite source conventions is defensible, but the repository still exposes a general discount-rate setting that rescales only mortality conversions, VQALY, and the frontier while leaving direct cost-per-QALY anchors fixed; that control must be labeled as a partial conversion sensitivity rather than a common discount-rate scenario.
6 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The methods now accurately state a shared realization draw and separate RCT-grade frontier credibility (lines 182–187), and the GiveWell-to-QALY conversion is identified as the model’s. Yet the results still say “the same realization and credibility discounts” (lines 562–565), contrary to src/msqaly/model.py, which independently draws frontier credibility; raw/realization-only variants also remain absent.
7 PARTIALLY RESOLVED Table 1 now correctly labels its $/QALY as center dollars divided by mean QALYs, a ratio of expectations (lines 218–221). The generated results/summary.md still displays nonadditive archetype medians in an ordering based on another statistic without the requested warning, so the artifact-level ambiguity remains.
8 RESOLVED Table 2 and the surrounding prose now unambiguously identify a deterministic post-estimation decomposition of mean archetype QALYs, with no regional effect or uncertainty estimated and geography affecting pricing only through routing (rendered lines 422–425, 513–516).
9 RESOLVED The manuscript now defines 4.9 as the median draw-level benefit–cost ratio and states that its 90% interval jointly reflects health-output and sampled HHS valuation uncertainty (lines 193–199).
10 PARTIALLY RESOLVED The interval is now called a probabilistic-sensitivity range rather than a confidence interval, major omitted inputs are listed, and Monte Carlo error is acknowledged (lines 193–199, 701–723). The requested compact dependence/fixed-input/statistic specification and an actual convergence or Monte Carlo-error calculation are still absent; “a few hundred” is asserted rather than shown.

New findings from the regression pass

  1. SEVERITY: MINOR
    LOCATION: paper/review/rendered.txt lines 76–80 and 700–706; paper/index.qmd line 311.
    Problem: The revision correctly changed the introduction to say the estimate is “not a lower bound on total wellbeing,” but the limitations still call it “a floor on total wellbeing.” The latter is methodologically false under strictly positive priors that exclude zero and harmful health effects, and it directly contradicts the newly revised framing.
    Fix: Replace “a floor on total wellbeing” with “a partial health-only accounting that omits both non-health benefits and possible harms,” or equivalent language consistent with the introduction.
