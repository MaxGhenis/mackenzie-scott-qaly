# Neutrality review — round 1

Reviewer: Claude (fable), neutrality referee. Read: rendered.txt, index.qmd, AGENTS.md.

## Assessment

This paper is unusually disciplined for its genre. It contains no policy prescriptions, never argues Scott should give differently, and builds an explicit anti-overreach architecture at each point of maximal risk: the QALY-lens caveat precedes any number, the geographic table is labeled a by-construction decomposition, the frontier counterfactual is immediately deflated as a marginal comparison with a cited diminishing-returns floor, and the conclusion states the number is a model output under stated priors, not a fact. Low-QALY causes are never disparaged; the treatment of Scott is descriptive and, where evaluative at all, favorable; the EA framing stays on the measurement side of the line; the personal-project disclaimer appears twice. The stance-sweep-plus-browser design operationalizes neutrality by handing the decisive judgment to the reader. Two items must be fixed before posting: a sentence labeling the headline decomposition "the paper's central fact," and the absence of a financial-relationship disclosure. Remaining findings are wording-level.

## Findings

1. MAJOR — §4.1: "The result is the paper's central fact: ..." labels a model output a fact, contradicting the conclusion and AGENTS.md. Fix: "central estimate/result", or attach "fact" only to the 4.8% dollar side.
2. MAJOR — author footnote/acknowledgments: no financial-conflict statement despite the author leading two nonprofits in the plausible grantee universe of a living donor. Fix: one sentence disclosing presence or absence of any funding relationship with Scott/Yield Giving.
3. MINOR — abstract + intro: factual voice for model outputs ("impact is", "produces", "documents"). Fix: "estimated impact", "accounts for", "estimates".
4. MINOR — "dwarfs every other modeling choice" (abstract, §5.1): intensifier + broader comparison class than quantified. Fix: "larger/exceeds the spread from any other modeling choice we test / any distributional parameter."
5. MINOR — §4.1 "1,200-fold spread": endpoints are assumption-tier buckets where realized $/QALY echoes the assumed near-zero credibility; lens caveat not restated at point of maximal risk. Fix: add clause noting the low end is partly the lens and assumption tier speaking; optionally lead with the health-to-health spread.
6. MINOR — §4.1 "central empirical claim of the effective-altruism literature": uncited characterization. Fix: cite canonical statements (e.g., Ord's moral-imperative essay; GiveWell CEA pages).
7. MINOR — §4.2 "plausibly under 20–40×... under any plausible account": speculative universals; "the honest statement" implies alternatives dishonest. Fix: tie to the cited GiveWell floor; "the defensible statement is directional... under the diminishing-returns accounts in the cited literature."
8. MINOR — §5.1 "would not survive a causal-inference seminar": rhetorical flourish; "claimed" loaded. Fix: "below randomized or strong quasi-experimental identification standards."
9. MINOR — §2.2 "she publishes better": evaluative praise. Fix: "something more granular."
10. MINOR — intro ¶1 "no published estimate prices the portfolio": priority claim without the "to my knowledge" qualifier used later. Fix: add qualifier.
11. MINOR — conclusion "the same machinery applies to any large giver": outruns the disclosure dependency. Fix: "any large giver with comparable gift-level disclosure — or, for foundations, the grant listings in their public filings."
12. MINOR — §2.2 "the disclosed two-thirds is representative": unconditional. Fix: "under the imputation model, ..."

## Strengths to preserve
The four scoping disclaimers at exactly the overreach points; zero prescriptive language; the stance sweep as structural neutrality; evenhanded modeling toward the subject (realization >1 on cited capacity evidence); genuine, specific limitations.
